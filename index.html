CAN I CHALLENGE YOU WITH SOMETHING THIS WEEK?
 

Have you ever caught yourself thinking...


"Why doesn't my team take ownership?"


"Why do I have to keep reminding everyone?"


"Why doesn't anyone think ahead?"

I'VE BEEN THERE

And if I'm honest...


Sometimes the problem wasn't my team.

IT WAS MY LEADERSHIP.

       

     

Not because I wasn't working hard.

     

       

But because I assumed people understood what I expected without me ever making those expectations crystal clear.


One of the biggest mistakes we make as childcare owners is promoting great employees without preparing them to think differently.

       

We hand them a new title.


A new salary.


A new office.


A new set of responsibilities.

 

 

But we forget the most important promotion...

 THEIR MINDSET

       

Right now, I'm walking through this with two of my own leaders.


One was recently promoted to Director.


The other to Assistant Director.


And this week, I shared a resource with them that completely reframes what leadership actually looks like.

Because a Director isn't just doing more work.


They're seeing the entire organization differently.


Instead of asking:

      .

"WHAT NEEDS TO HAPPEN TODAY?"

      .
They're asking...

• What will my team need next week?

• What could become a problem next month?

• What isn't on the calendar yet that needs to be?

• What systems need to exist so we stop solving the same problems over and over again?

      .

 THAT'S THE SHIFT 

The truth is...


You cannot continue wearing every hat while expecting your business to grow.
When you're constantly switching between teacher, enrollment coordinator, HR manager, maintenance person, marketer, family liaison, and CEO...

 

      .
Your thinking becomes fragmented.

And fragmented thinking usually creates fragmented results.

• Enrollment stalls.

• Staff become frustrated.

• Families feel inconsistency.

• And the owner becomes exhausted.

      .

     

Growth doesn't happen because you do more.
 

It happens because you build leaders who can think beyond today's to-do list.

     

      .

      .

SO TODAY, I WANT TO DO SOMETHING DIFFERENT.

      .

I'm sharing one of the actual leadership resources I created for my newly promoted Director and Assistant Director.

This may help YOU as the Owner and/or Director, as a Leader, etc.  I think it applies to all of us in leadership and there's a takeaway and reflection opportunity for everyone.

 A FIELD GUIDE FOR THE TRANSITION INTO DIRECTOR/LEADER

      .

Inside, you'll discover:


✔ The mindset shift every new Director must make


✔ Why leading a center is completely different from supporting one


✔ The difference between reacting to problems and anticipating them


✔ A practical framework to help leaders think like the conductor of an orchestra instead of just playing one instrument

      .

It's one of the most important leadership conversations we've had this year, and I hope it serves you and your team too.
